The Property

A Living Monument to Florentine Hospitality

Palazzo Venezia Firenze occupies a beautifully restored fifteenth-century palazzo in the heart of Florence's Oltrarno quarter, a short walk from the Ponte Vecchio and the Uffizi Gallery. The building's original stone arches, hand-painted coffered ceilings, and terracotta floors have been preserved with meticulous care, while every modern comfort has been woven seamlessly into the historic fabric.

The palazzo has housed artists, merchants, and diplomats across its long history. Today it receives guests from around the world who come to Florence in search of beauty, culture, and the unhurried pleasures of the Italian table. The property is family-owned and operates with the quiet conviction that a hotel should feel like a private residence borrowed for a few exceptional days.

Accommodation

Rooms & Suites

Each of the palazzo's twenty-two rooms and suites is individually furnished with antiques sourced from Florentine dealers and original artwork commissioned from local artists. No two rooms are alike. Beds are dressed in hand-embroidered Pratesi linen; bathrooms are finished in Carrara marble with fittings by Fantini. The building's thick stone walls keep rooms cool in summer and warm in the cooler months without mechanical assistance.

Food & Wine

Dining at Palazzo Venezia

Breakfast is served each morning in the cortile garden or, on cooler days, beneath the palazzo's vaulted dining room. The kitchen sources its produce from small Tuscan farms with which the hotel has maintained direct relationships for decades. Bread, pastries, and fresh pasta are made on the premises.

The Enoteca Venezia opens each evening at six o'clock and offers a selection of over two hundred Tuscan wines by the glass. The sommelier is available to guide guests through lesser-known appellations — the Maremma, Montecucco, Morellino di Scansano — alongside the celebrated Brunello and Chianti Classico producers that have defined Tuscan viticulture.

Light dining — cold cuts, aged cheeses, bruschetta, seasonal soups — is available in the enoteca until ten o'clock. The hotel does not operate a full-service restaurant; instead, the concierge team maintains close relationships with twelve neighbourhood trattorias and can reserve tables on a guest's behalf within the hour.

Position & Access

In the Heart of the City

The palazzo sits on a narrow side street in the Oltrarno — the quieter, artisan side of the Arno — within walking distance of Florence's principal monuments. The neighbourhood is home to the city's finest bookbinders, restorers, goldsmiths, and frame-makers, and the streets retain a texture that the north bank surrendered to commerce some decades ago.
