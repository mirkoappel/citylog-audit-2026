About the Hotel

The Hotel Bristol Wien stands as one of Vienna's most distinguished addresses, occupying a landmark position directly opposite the Vienna State Opera on the grand Ringstrasse boulevard. Built in 1892, the hotel has welcomed emperors, statesmen, and artists for over a century, maintaining a standard of refinement that defines Viennese hospitality.

Restored with meticulous care, the Bristol combines the grandeur of the imperial era — ornate stucco ceilings, hand-laid marble floors, crystal chandeliers — with contemporary comforts that meet the expectations of today's discerning traveller. A member of the Leading Hotels of the World, it is consistently rated among Europe's finest urban hotels.

Location & Surroundings

Positioned at Kärntner Ring 1, the Bristol places guests at the intersection of Vienna's cultural and commercial life. The Kunsthistorisches Museum, the Musikverein concert hall, and the Albertina gallery are all within a ten-minute walk. The historic first district — the Innere Stadt — with its cobblestone lanes, coffeehouses, and the Stephansdom cathedral, is easily reached on foot or by the U4 underground from nearby Karlsplatz.

For opera lovers, the proximity to the Wiener Staatsoper is unparalleled: residents can cross the street for a performance and return on foot within minutes. Concierge staff maintain a permanent allocation of premium seats and can arrange pre-opera dinners timed to curtain.

Rooms & Suites

All 150 rooms and suites are individually appointed, drawing on a palette of warm golds, deep burgundies, and ivory — a conscious echo of the Habsburg interior tradition. Each features high ceilings, tall windows overlooking either the Ringstrasse or the hotel's private inner courtyard, and bespoke Viennese furniture crafted by local artisans.

Dining

Korb & Krone — Main Restaurant

The hotel's signature restaurant occupies the original Wilhelmine-era dining hall, its vaulted ceiling and gilt columns intact. Executive Chef Magdalena Huber presents a menu rooted in Viennese and broader Austrian tradition — Tafelspitz, Wiener Schnitzel vom Kalb, and seasonal game — alongside a carefully curated wine list of over 400 labels from the Wachau, Burgenland, and Steiermark regions. Open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

The Bristol Bar

A clubroom atmosphere of dark walnut panelling and green leather banquettes. The bar programme focuses on classic Viennese cocktails — the Sacher Sour, the Ringstrasse Negroni — alongside an exceptional selection of aged Austrian schnaps and single-malt Scotch. Light plates and a cheese trolley are available until midnight.

Café Ringside

The hotel's all-day café opens directly onto the boulevard terrace in warmer months. Viennese Frühstück, afternoon Jause with house-baked Kipferl and tortes, and a rotating selection of Tagesmenüs from noon. Beloved by both guests and local regulars.
