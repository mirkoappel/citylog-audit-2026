Château Bellevue

Estate Hotel & Wine Country Retreat · Saint-Émilion Grand Cru Appellation · Bordeaux, France

The Estate

Château Bellevue occupies a restored 18th-century manor house at the heart of the Saint-Émilion Grand Cru appellation, surrounded by twelve hectares of classified vineyards. The property has been managed by the Beaumont family for four generations, combining the traditions of Bordelais viticulture with the hospitality of a small luxury hotel.

The château offers fourteen individually designed guest rooms and suites, each named after a historic Bordeaux vintage. Original stone walls, oak-beamed ceilings, and antique furnishings are complemented by contemporary comforts throughout. A heated outdoor pool, private wine cellar, and estate restaurant make Château Bellevue a complete destination for guests seeking immersion in the culture and landscape of the Bordeaux wine country.

Dining: La Table du Château

The estate restaurant draws directly from the château's kitchen garden and partnerships with local producers across the Gironde. Head chef Antoine Renard trained in Lyon before returning to Bordeaux to develop a menu that reflects the seasonal rhythms of the region. Wine pairings are curated by the resident sommelier from the estate cellar and a selection of neighbouring Saint-Émilion and Pomerol producers.

The restaurant is open to hotel guests and outside visitors for dinner Wednesday through Sunday, and for lunch on weekends. The private dining room accommodates up to twelve guests for exclusive events with dedicated service.

Guest Experiences

The château offers curated wine country activities throughout the stay: private cellar visits and vertical tastings, guided vineyard walks with the estate winemaker, cycling routes through the appellation, and day excursions to Saint-Émilion village and the surrounding classified estates. Harvest season visits between September and October offer the opportunity to participate in the grape harvest alongside the estate team.
